How to read this calculator

01

Your vendor price already includes their flavouring cost. Since they sell you finished, flavoured makhana, your own costs are simply packaging and transport — there's no separate seasoning or roasting step on your side to account for.

02

Different flavours can have very different vendor prices. Chocolate or cheese-coated makhana usually costs more per kg than a simple roasted version, because the vendor's own ingredient and process costs are higher. The comparison table lets you set a different £/kg for each flavour.

03

Net weight drives your makhana cost directly. A 100g pack needs exactly 0.1kg of flavoured makhana at the vendor's £/kg rate — if you ever change pack size, this cost scales proportionally, so re-check your channel prices whenever net weight changes.

04

E-commerce and retail still need separate prices. Your e-commerce price is what the customer pays you directly. Your wholesale price is what you invoice a retailer — lower, because they add their own markup. Check the shelf price stays sensible for a snack before locking in your wholesale rate.

05

Re-check pricing whenever your vendor changes a flavour's price. A vendor might raise the price on one flavour (say, if cheese or chocolate ingredient costs rise) without changing others — update only that flavour's £/kg rather than assuming all four move together.
